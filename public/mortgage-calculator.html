Understanding Your Mortgage Options

Choosing the right mortgage is one of the biggest financial decisions you'll make. A Fixed-Rate Mortgage guarantees that your interest rate and monthly principal and interest (P&I) payments will remain the same for the entire life of the loan. This provides predictability and protects you from rising interest rates.

An Adjustable-Rate Mortgage (ARM) typically offers a lower initial interest rate for a fixed period (such as 5 or 7 years), after which the rate adjusts periodically based on market conditions. This can be a smart choice if you plan to sell the home or refinance before the fixed period ends, but it carries the risk of higher payments if rates rise.

The Power of Extra Payments

Making extra payments toward your mortgage principal can drastically reduce the total amount of interest you pay over the life of the loan and help you become debt-free years sooner. Because interest is calculated on your remaining balance, every extra dollar you pay reduces the principal, which in turn reduces the interest charged in all subsequent months.

  • Bi-weekly Payments: Paying half your monthly mortgage every two weeks results in 26 half-payments, or 13 full payments a year—effectively adding one extra monthly payment annually.
  • Extra Monthly Payments: Even adding $50 or $100 extra to your regular monthly payment can shave years off your loan term and save thousands in interest.
  • Lump Sum Payments: Using tax refunds, bonuses, or other windfalls to make a single extra yearly payment is an excellent way to accelerate your payoff timeline without affecting your monthly budget.
